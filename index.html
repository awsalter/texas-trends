Price Pressures

How fast are prices rising? This section tracks the Consumer Price Index (CPI) — the broadest measure of what everyday goods and services cost — for the United States, Texas, and the Dallas-Fort Worth metro area. All figures are indexed to January 2020 = 100, so a value of 120 means prices are 20% higher than at the start of 2020. The dotted line shows what prices would look like if inflation had stayed at the Federal Reserve's 2% annual target.

Money Matters

This section tracks the Federal Reserve's interest rate policy. The "real" federal funds rate is the Fed's benchmark rate adjusted for inflation — the key lever the Fed uses to speed up or slow down the economy. We compare it to two independent estimates of the "natural rate of interest": the rate that keeps the economy growing steadily without causing inflation. When the real rate is above the natural rate, monetary policy is tight. When it's below, policy is loose. Getting this balance right is the Fed's central challenge.
